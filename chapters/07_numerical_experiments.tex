\chapter{Numerical experiments}

This chapter presents the experimental setup and results for each of the three studies comprising this thesis.

\section{Study 1: Uncertainty Estimation via Token-Wise Entropy}

\subsection{Experimental Setup}

\textbf{Dataset}: We use the MMLU-Pro benchmark~\cite{wang2024mmlu}, which contains approximately 12,000 challenging multiple-choice questions with 10 answer choices across 14 diverse domains including Biology, Chemistry, Computer Science, Economics, Engineering, Health, History, Law, Math, Philosophy, Physics, Psychology, Business, and Other.

\textbf{Models}: We evaluate four decoder-only large language models:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Phi-4~\cite{abdin2024phi}
  \item Mistral-Small-24B-Base-2501~\cite{jiang2023mistral}
  \item Qwen-2.5-1.5B~\cite{qwen2024}
  \item Qwen-2.5-72B~\cite{qwen2024}
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Metrics}: We measure the predictive power of entropy features using ROC-AUC for distinguishing correct from incorrect answers, stratified by subject domain and reasoning requirement.

Key findings:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Entropy is an effective predictor of answer correctness in knowledge-dependent domains (Biology ROC-AUC 0.83 for Qwen-72B), but performance decreases for reasoning-heavy domains (Math ROC-AUC 0.55 for Phi-4).
  \item Larger models show better calibration: Qwen-72B achieves overall ROC-AUC of 0.77 compared to 0.52 for Mistral. Calibration analysis (Figure~\ref{fig:calibration}) reveals systematic overconfidence across all models, particularly in high-certainty regimes.
  \item Low entropy is a better predictor when no reasoning is required (ROC-AUC 0.79 for Qwen-72B) compared to high-reasoning questions (0.73), suggesting entropy primarily captures \textit{knowledge uncertainty} rather than \textit{reasoning difficulty}.
  \item The Model-as-Judge (MASJ) approach performs near-randomly with ROC-AUC values around 0.49--0.57, indicating it captures different aspects of an LLM's confidence that are irrelevant to question complexity.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Study 2: Language Steering in Latent Space}

\subsection{Experimental Setup}

\textbf{Models}: We evaluate two multilingual models:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Qwen2.5-1.5B (28 layers)
  \item Llama-3.2-1B (16 layers)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Languages}: We test four language pairs: English--Chinese, English--Spanish, English--Russian, and English--Hindi.

\textbf{Datasets}: We use the Flores Plus dataset~\cite{nllb2024} with 50 parallel samples per language for PCA fitting and 100 for validation. For steering evaluation, we use 2,467 TED Talks samples with artificial code-switching (texts begin in English and switch mid-sentence to the target language).

  \item English--Spanish shows slightly lower accuracy (0.96) due to these languages being more closely related within the Indo-European family.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Study 3: Complexity-Aware Fine-Tuning}

\subsection{Experimental Setup}

\textbf{Datasets}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item MMLU-Pro: multiple-choice question answering across 14 domains ($\sim$12,000 examples)
  \item GSM8K: 8.5K grade school math word problems~\cite{cobbe2021gsm8k}
  \item MedMCQA: large-scale medical entrance exam questions~\cite{pmlr-v174-pal22a}
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Models}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Student models: Qwen2.5-3B, Phi-4-Mini, LLama-3.2-3B
  \item Teacher model ensemble: DeepSeek-V3-0324, Qwen-3-235B, Llama-4-Maverick
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Baselines}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{SFT}: Standard supervised fine-tuning on all data without complexity split
  \item \textbf{Curriculum}: Curriculum-based SFT (3 epochs easy, 3 medium, 4 hard)
  \item \textbf{Distillation}: Full chain-of-thought distillation on all data
  \item \textbf{Alternative}: SFT on hard data, then CoT distillation on easy/medium data
\end{itemize}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Our complexity-aware pipeline matches or outperforms full distillation while using 79--82\% less training data (tokens processed).
  \item After 20 epochs (Figure~\ref{fig:training_curves}), the pipeline surpasses all baselines: Qwen-3B achieves 0.52 vs.\ 0.50 (distillation) and 0.39 (SFT); Phi-4-Mini achieves 0.64 vs.\ 0.63 (distillation) and 0.51 (SFT).
  \item The method generalizes across datasets: on GSM8K, LLama-3B achieves 0.82 (pipeline) vs.\ 0.79 (distillation) and 0.13 (SFT).
  \item The pipeline shows a continued uptrend in training accuracy, while full distillation plateaus, suggesting better utilization of training data.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Sensitivity Study}

\textbf{Complexity metric selection.} We compare single-token answer entropy against MASJ reasoning score and cross-entropy as complexity metrics. Single-token entropy yields the best ROC-AUC (0.72 for Qwen-3B without domain split) and provides clearer separation between easy and hard examples during SFT training. MASJ reasoning scores achieve ROC-AUC around 0.50--0.57, making them unsuitable for complexity estimation.

\textbf{Curriculum learning fragility.} Standard curriculum learning (easy $\rightarrow$ medium $\rightarrow$ hard ordering with SFT) shows extreme sensitivity to hyperparameters. Qwen-3B plateaus at lower accuracy with more training epochs, while Phi-4-Mini quickly overfits, demonstrating that naive complexity-aware ordering without differentiated training strategies is insufficient.

\section{Summary of Results}

Across all three studies, we demonstrate that signals from the model's forward pass provide valuable information for addressing key challenges in LLM deployment:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Uncertainty estimation}: Token-wise entropy achieves ROC-AUC up to 0.83 for predicting answer correctness in knowledge-dependent domains, while MASJ performs near-randomly.
  \item \textbf{Language control}: PCA-identified language directions enable 25--55\% reduction in distributional divergence and 63--95\% reduction in Code-Switching Index in generated text across four language pairs.
  \item \textbf{Training efficiency}: Complexity-aware fine-tuning matches or outperforms full distillation while using 79--82\% less training data, generalizing across three datasets and three model architectures.
\end{enumerate}

These results validate our central hypothesis that accessible signals from forward passes encode sufficient information to improve reliability, control, and efficiency without requiring architectural modifications.
